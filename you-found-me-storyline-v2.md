# 💌 You Found Me — Polished Storyline
### *Childhood sweethearts. A slow burn. A quiet island. And a debt of love long overdue.*

---

## 🌅 Prologue — Before the Boat

*The screen is black. No music yet. Just the sound of distant waves.*

Text appears, one line at a time. Slow. Deliberate.

---

> *There is a certain kind of lost*
> *that has no map.*

> *Not lost in a place.*
> *Lost in yourself.*
> *The kind where you stop recognizing*
> *the face in the mirror.*

> *That was him.*
> *For a long time — that was him.*

---

*A pause. Then:*

> *And then she walked back into his life.*
> *Not loudly. Not with fanfare.*
> *The way light comes through a curtain.*
> *Quietly. Gradually.*
> *Until you realize the whole room has changed.*

---

> *She saw him.*
> *Not who he was pretending to be.*
> *Not who he used to be.*
> *Him. The real one.*
> *The one he'd almost forgotten existed.*

---

> *He didn't know it then,*
> *but she saved him.*

---

*Another pause. Then, softer:*

> *And then one morning —*
> *she was gone.*

> *No fight. No warning.*
> *Just a letter on the table.*
> *And two things inside.*

---

*The screen slowly illuminates — a close-up of a worn envelope.*
*A hand reaches in. Pulls out a folded map. And a small card.*

*The card reads, in her handwriting:*

> *"X: 150, Z: -300*
> *If you ever loved me... find me."*

---

*The prologue ends.*
*The screen fades to the dock.*

---

## 🌊 Act 1 — Setting Sail

*Dusk. A foggy harbor. The kind of quiet that feels intentional.*

*A small wooden boat bobs at the end of a pier.*
*There is no one else here.*
*There is only the water, and the mist, and somewhere beyond it — her.*

*The player presses any key.*

*The boat moves. Slowly at first.*
*Then the fog thickens, and the dock disappears behind you,*
*and there is nothing left but forward.*

*On the horizon — barely visible —*
*a shadow of land. Small. Secluded.*
*The kind of island that doesn't show up on maps*
*unless someone wants you to find it.*

*As the shore grows closer, the title drifts in:*

```
        💌  Y O U   F O U N D   M E  💌
```

*The boat touches the sand.*
*The player steps off.*

*The island is still.*
*Alive, but still.*
*Like it's been waiting.*

## 🏝️ Act 2 — The Island

*The world opens.*

*Pixel-art. 16-bit. But somehow — warm.*
*Wildflowers along the shore. A dense forest ahead.*
*A waterfall you can hear before you can see.*
*A winding dirt path, like someone walked it enough times to leave a mark.*

*Most of the map is covered in fog.*
*Only the space around you is clear.*
*As if understanding — like love — only comes*
*one step at a time.*

*The coordinate tracker appears in the top right corner, quiet and minimal:*

```
X: 0    Z: 0
```

*Somewhere on this island:*  `X: 150, Z: -300`

*The journey begins.*

## ⚡ The Wings

*Near the spawn point — half-hidden in tall grass near the shore —*
*a pair of wings rests on the ground.*

*White. Feathered. Glowing faintly.*
*They look like something from a dream you almost remembered.*

*A small note is folded beneath them:*

> *"For when walking isn't fast enough.*
> *You were always worth running to."*

*When collected — the player lifts.*
*Movement becomes flight.*
*Water and trees no longer matter.*
*The island opens up.*

*It feels, strangely, like relief.*

---

## 💎 Act 4 — You Found Me

*The player reaches `X: 150, Z: -300`.*

*A small clearing.*
*One tree at the center — older than the others.*
*And beneath it: freshly turned earth.*
*And a small hand-painted sign, leaning slightly:*

```
        [ For you. If you came this far. ]
```

*The character stops moving.*

*The digging animation begins — slow, quiet, deliberate.*
*Golden pixel particles scatter upward like dust catching light.*

*Then —*

*Something catches the light.*
*A corner of gold, just beneath the surface.*

## 💌 Act 5 — The Letter

*The envelope rises.*

*Golden trim. A wax seal shaped like a small heart.*
*It floats upward — slowly — filling the screen.*

*The world goes completely quiet.*

*The screen dims.*
*A soft click.*
*The seal cracks — cleanly — in two.*
*The flap folds open.*

*A parchment slides out.*

*The pixel font dissolves.*
*What replaces it is warm, elegant, unhurried.*

*And the letter begins:*

---

```
                        To Hyacinth,


        I've been thinking about how to start this
        for a long time.

        And every time, I come back to the same truth:

        You found me first.

        I was lost — really lost —
        the kind of lost that doesn't announce itself.
        I just woke up one day and didn't recognize
        anything around me. Including myself.

        And then there was you.

        You didn't fix me.
        You didn't try to.
        You just... saw me.
        The real me, underneath all of it.
        And you stayed anyway.

        Do you know how rare that is?
        Do you know what it did to me —
        to be seen like that?

        I went to this island because I needed to know
        if I was worth finding.
        If you'd cross water for me
        the way I'd cross anything for you.

        And here you are.

        Here you are.

        One month.
        It sounds so small when you say it out loud.
        But I have lived more in this one month with you
        than in years before it.

        Thank you for the late nights.
        For the silences that never felt empty.
        For finding me — even when I didn't leave
        very good directions.

        Daghang salamat, my love.
        For an amazing month.
        For being my person.
        For finding me —

        the way I once found you.


                                Always yours,
                                     — [Your Name] 💛
```

## ✍️ Act 6 — The Reply

*After the last word types itself onto the page —*
*the confetti falls.*
*Gold and white. Slow.*

*The music swells.*

*Then — after a breath — the confetti stills.*
*The parchment remains on screen.*
*And at the bottom, something new appears:*

*A blank line. A blinking cursor. And above it, in small elegant text:*

> *"Your turn."*

*The player can type a short reply.*
*Whatever they want.*
*As long or as short as it needs to be.*

*When they press send —*

*The reply folds itself into a small paper boat.*
*It drifts off the screen.*
*Toward the water.*
*Toward her.*

*The screen fades slowly to the island — now fully revealed, fog completely gone.*
*Every corner visible.*
*Every path clear.*

*And in the distance, just at the edge of the shore —*
*a small figure.*
*Standing.*
*Watching the water.*

*Waiting.*

*The screen holds for a moment.*

*Then — gently — it fades to black.*

*One last line appears:*

> *"She found you first.*
> *You found her back.*
> *That's the whole story."*
